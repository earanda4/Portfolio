About Me

My name is Enrique, and I am currently a Fourth-Year at University of California, San Diego pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Science and minor in General Biology. I'm passionate in Machine Learning, Cybersecurity, Computational Biology, and Software Engineering.

My passion for computer science stems from the fourth grade, where the Gifted and Talented Education (G.A.T.E.) program introduced me to coding. The programs and games I was able to make in Scratch were mindblowing to me and allowed me to express my creativity and indulge in my curiosity. This enthusiasm carried on to middle school and high school where I joined the Robotics club and became part of the software team for our robot. After acing my AP Computer Science exams, I decided I wanted to major in computer science and pursue what I love for my career. Now, with the skills I’ve practiced in university, I’m once again able to express my creativity through projects such as this website, and I anticipate learning more throughout my career.

My Projects

Custom RISC Processor

Collaborated with a teammate to develop a Reduced Instruction Set Computer (RISC) processor under specific constraints such as fixed 9-bit instructions and 8 general purpose registers storing only 8 bits of data. In one month, we assembled components in SystemVerilog for the processor and created an assembler that would convert our ARM functions into machine code that our processor would then use to provide output.

Technologies: Python, ARM, Assembly, SystemVerilog, C

We extracted features such as pitch, duration, and note sequences using miditoolkit, and trained the model to predict tokenized note sequences with a language modeling objective.
